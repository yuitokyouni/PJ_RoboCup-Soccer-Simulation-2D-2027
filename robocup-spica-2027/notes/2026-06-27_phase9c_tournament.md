## Findings

1. **REV is the strongest Spica edition.** Symmetrization closes the
   gap vs Vanilla from -1.00 (ORIG) to -0.50 (REV) — half the deficit.
   The Y-axis bias in the original F433 conf was the larger leak than
   the kickoff configuration.

2. **Defensive kickoff > aggressive kickoff** in head-to-head: REV
   beats MERGE by +0.25 (n=4). MERGE's leakier defense costs more
   goals than its extra offense scores. This matches the user
   feedback "守備ザル".

3. **All Spica editions still lose to Vanilla** at n=4. REV is the
   closest at -0.50 but the floor isn't broken. Tournament n is too
   small (4 per pair) for tight statistical claims; the directional
   signal is clear though.

## Recommendation

Use REV (Y-symmetric F433 + defensive kickoff) as the production
strategy. The other two editions can be retired.

Next step (open): confirm REV vs Vanilla at n=30 balanced. Tournament
n=4 measured -0.50, Phase 9 final at n=30 was -0.77 on ORIG-equivalent
— so REV should sit around -0.4 to -0.6 in a proper n=30 eval if the
signal is real.
